Who is Who?

The Main Characters

Maria

Maria Von Trapp is the main protagonist. She is a free-spirited and adventurous young woman who becomes a governess for the von Trapp family. She has a passion for music and helps the children rediscover their love for singing.


Captain von Trapp

Captain von Trapp is the male lead. He is a stern and disciplined man who runs his household like a military operation. He initially struggles to connect with his children but eventually learns to show them affection and love with the help of Maria.

Other Central Characters Mother Abbess

Mother Abbess is the head of the abbey where Maria becomes a governess. She is wise, kind, and compassionate, and serves as a mentor to Maria, helping her to find her path in life.


Max Detweiler

Max Detweiler is a music promoter and friend of the Von Trapp family. He is a charming and opportunistic man who sees the potential for profit in the family's musical talent.


Baroness Elsa von Schraeder

The Baroness is a wealthy and sophisticated woman who is engaged to Captain Von Trapp at the beginning of the story. She is cultured and refined, but ultimately lacks the warmth and compassion of Maria. Her engagement with Captain Von Trapp is eventually broken off.

The Sound of Music

Based on the true story of the Von Trapp Family singers, The Sound of Music is a classic musical movie. Directed and produced by Robert Wise, The Sound of Music debuted on the big screens in 1965. It stars Julie Andrews as Maria, a young Austrian postulant (at a nunnery) who is sent to the home of a retired naval officer to be the governess to his seven children.

The Sound of Music Poster




"The happiest sound in all the world"

The line "the happiest sound in all the world" is from the song "My Favorite Things" in the musical "The Sound of Music". The song was written by Richard Rodgers and Oscar Hammerstein II, and it is sung by the character Maria von Trapp as a way to comfort the children during a thunderstorm. The line refers to things that Maria lists as her favorite things. The song became a popular hit and has been covered by many artists over the years. The line "the happiest sound in all the world" has become a well-known phrase associated with the musical and is often used to describe the joy and happiness that the story and its music bring to audiences.

The Real Story

The real-life story behind the musical is based on the memoirs of Maria von Trapp, a former nun who was hired by the widowed Captain von Trapp to tutor his seven children. Maria eventually fell in love with the Captain and they married in 1927. The family formed a singing group and toured throughout Austria and other parts of Europe, becoming famous for their harmonious vocal performances. When the Nazis annexed Austria in 1938, the von Trapps fled to Switzerland and later settled in the United States, where they continued to perform and eventually became American citizens.


Accolades and Awards

  • Two Golden Globe Awards: Best Motion Picture and Best Actress
  • Directors Guild of America Award for Outstanding Directorial Achievement
  • Writers Guild of America Award for Best Written American Musical
  • The American Film Institute (AFI) listed it as the fifty-fifth greatest American film of all time
  • AFI also listed it as the fourth greated film musical
  • In 2001, the United States Library of Congress selected it for preservation in the National Film Registry
